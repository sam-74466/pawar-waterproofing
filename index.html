Leak sealing

PU Injection (Cracks & Joints)

Stops active water seepage in basements, lift-pits, tanks and slabs by pressure-injecting expanding PU resin.

Process — 5 steps
  • Mark leak lines → drill packer points at 45°
  • Fix mechanical packers and flush
  • Inject hydrophilic/hydrophobic PU under pressure
  • Resin expands and blocks water pathways
  • Remove packers → patch and finish
Materials we use

PU resin (fast/slow set), mechanical packers, injection pump, polymer-modified mortar for finishing.

FAQs
  • How long does it last? Many years if movement is minimal; cracks that move may need a top sealant joint.
  • Can you inject during active leakage? Yes, we use fast-foaming resin for live leaks.
  • Will it stain the wall? Holes are patched; only tiny dots remain.
Structural

Epoxy Grouting (Honeycombs & Voids)

Binds cracked RCC and fills voids to restore strength around beams, columns and slabs.

Process — 5 steps
  • Crack mapping & core check
  • Surface sealing and port fixing
  • Low-viscosity epoxy injection
  • Curing & port removal
  • Reprofiling and finishing
Materials

LV epoxy systems, surface sealer, injection ports, grinder, PPE.

FAQs
  • Is it waterproofing? It’s structural. For leakage we combine with PU or surface systems.
  • Any downtime? 24–48 hours typical.
Below grade

Basement Waterproofing

Stops ground water ingress through retaining walls and raft junctions using injection + negative-side coatings.

Process — 5 steps
  • Leak diagnosis & salt removal
  • PU injection at active seams
  • Crack chasing & polymer mortar
  • Crystal growth/elastic coating (2–3 coats)
  • Fillet coves & protective top coat
Materials

PU resin, crystalline slurry, elastomeric coat, primers, polymer mortar.

FAQs
  • Will the salt bloom return? Not after proper neutralisation and coating system.
  • Do you need outside excavation? Usually no; we treat from inside.
